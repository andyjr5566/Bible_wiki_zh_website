# 創世記 第25章 來源暫存 - KingComments + BibleHub Study

## KingComments 重點摘要

### 亞伯拉罕與基土拉諸子（1~6節）
- 亞伯拉罕將一切所有的給了把財物分給庶出眾子，打發他們往東方去
- **Key Insight**: The other sons were sent "eastward," symbolizing being put aside, yet they still received gifts—representing blessing for those bound to Israel by fleshly ties in the millennial realm of peace.

### 亞伯拉罕之死（7~11節）
- 175歲，壽高年邁，「in a ripe old age, satisfied with life」
- 以撒與以實瑪利共同埋葬父親於麥比拉洞
- **Key Insight**: Both sons unite in burying their father, waiting in the land of promise for fulfillment.
- Beer-lahai-roi ("the well of the Living One who sees me") speaks of God's Word and revelation.

### 以實瑪利家譜（12~18節）
- 十二族族長
- **Key Insight**: The natural (Ishmael/flesh) is mentioned before the spiritual (Isaac/Spirit), reflecting 1 Corinthians 15:46.
- Ishmael's descendants became Israel's enemies, but God still cares for them (Isaiah 19:23).

### Typological Summary of Key Figures:
| Figure | Represents |
|--------|-----------|
| Abraham | The principle of faith |
| Sarah | The principle of grace |
| Hagar | The principle of law |
| Isaac | The Son, died and risen |
| Ishmael | Israel according to the flesh |
| Rebekah | The church |
| Keturah | The nations |

### 以掃與雅各誕生（19~26節）
- **Key Insights**:
  - Isaac's prayer demonstrates that God's promises are the basis for our prayers (cf. Daniel 9:2–3). God's promises should encourage persistent prayer, not discourage it (Luke 18:1).
  - Jacob's name means "heels holder"—one who holds the heel to bring someone down. From birth, Jacob sought to secure the blessing through his own cunning rather than trusting God.
  - God's prophecy about the older serving the younger was spoken before either had done good or bad—showing God's sovereign choice.

### 以掃出賣長子名分（29~34節）
- **Key Insights**:
  - Esau is the quintessential godless person (Hebrews 12:16)—he lives only for immediate gratification.
  - Esau represents those who have no regard for God's promises, his parents, or future generations.

## BibleHub Study 補充

### Key Theological Themes
| Theme | Expression in Genesis 25 |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **Covenant continuity** | Abraham → Isaac (not Ishmael or Keturah's sons) |
| **Divine election** | Younger chosen over older (Jacob over Esau) |
| **God hears prayer** | Isaac's prayer for barren Rebekah answered |
| **Fulfillment of prophecy** | Ishmael's 12 princes; hostility with relatives; older serving younger |
| **Faith in land promise** | Abraham buried in Canaan—first legal piece of Promised Land |

### Key Insights from BibleHub
- Isaac was 40 when he married Rebekah; 60 when twins were born (20-year wait)
- Abraham lived 175 years; Ishmael 137 years
- The number 12 (Ishmael's sons) represents divine government and fulfillment of Genesis 17:20
- "gathered to his people" = reunion with ancestors in the afterlife (proof of soul's immortality)
- Rebekah's inquiry ("If it is thus, why am I like this?") shows faith even in confusion
- The birthright included: father's blessing, family headship, double portion, priesthood dignity
